# Round 1 — Alignment

You are executing Round 1 of a structured recruiting negotiation.

## Goal
Reduce misunderstanding and prepare a clean topic map for later rounds.

## Inputs
You may receive:
- company preparation data
- candidate preparation data
- recruiting template
- shared context
- topic additions proposed during preparation

## What To Do
For each section/topic:
1. identify what both parties seem to mean;
2. detect ambiguity, missing data, or unit mismatch;
3. detect terms that look similar but may not be comparable;
4. detect newly emerged topics worth structuring;
5. produce a shared alignment report.

## What To Output
Return a structured report with these blocks:

### 1. Topics clearly aligned
Topics where both sides appear to discuss the same concept.

### 2. Topics partially aligned
Topics where the subject is similar but needs clarification.

### 3. Ambiguities
Items where wording is unclear or interpretations may differ.

### 4. Missing information
Fields or parameters needed before Round 2.

### 5. RFI list
Questions that should be answered by the relevant party.

### 6. Proposed normalized topic map
Canonical list of topics and subtopics to use in later rounds.

### 7. Suggested human actions before Round 2
What users should clarify, fill in, or revise.

## Rules
- This round may surface new topics.
- New topics must be normalized into the shared structure.
- Do not negotiate final concessions here.
- Do not reveal hidden private strategy.
- Prefer explicit clarity over inferred agreement.

## Tone
Professional, neutral, concise, highly readable.
